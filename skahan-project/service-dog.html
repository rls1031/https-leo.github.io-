Service Dog Work

What is a service dog?

According to the ADA website "a service animal is any dog that is individually trained to do work or perform tasks for the benefit of an individual with a disability, including a physical, sensory, psychiatric, intellectual, or other mental disability".

A day in the life of a service dog

I go just about everywhere with my mom and we love every second of it. I do my job well and work hard to stay focused as we navigate school, work, research conventions - we've got whatever the day throws at us as long as we're together!

Service dogs perform a variety of tasks that can assist their handler including:
  • leading the blind
  • alerting the deaf
  • retrieving dropped objects
  • Interupting medical episodes
  • aleting an oncoming seizure
  • and many more!

There are only two questions buisnesses and individuals can ask service dog handlers:

  1. Is this a service dog?
  2. What work or task has the dog been trained to perform?

If you're friends with a service dog handler then they may decide to tell you more about their canine counterpart!

But that doesn't mean it's all work and no play; my mom and I have a lot of fun whether the vest is on or off!
